5.6SDE3 Expectations

What Differentiates SDE3 from SDE2

Career
DimensionSDE2SDE3
ScopeFeature/component levelSystem/cross-team level
AmbiguityExecutes well-defined tasksDefines the tasks, breaks down ambiguous problems
InfluenceWithin teamAcross teams, sets technical direction
DesignImplements designsAuthors design docs, defines architecture
MentoringHelps teammatesFormally mentors, grows SDE1/2s
OperationsParticipates in on-callImproves operational practices, prevents incidents
Code QualityWrites good codeRaises the bar for the whole team (standards, reviews)

Production Insight

In FAANG interviews for SDE3, interviewers look for: (1) Can you break down ambiguous problems? (2) Do you consider multiple approaches and articulate trade-offs? (3) Can you influence without authority? (4) Do you think about long-term maintainability and team velocity, not just solving today's problem?

Worked STAR Story — Leading a Cross-Team Initiative

Story

Signals: drives technical direction, influence across team boundaries, raises the bar — the core SDE3 differentiators above.

  • S: "Our checkout flow depended on four services owned by three teams. A shared p99 latency regression kept recurring and no single team owned it, so each incident bounced between on-calls for hours."
  • T: "No one had asked me to fix it and I had no authority over those teams, but it was hurting conversion. I took it on."
  • A: "I instrumented the full request path with distributed tracing to make the bottleneck visible with data instead of finger-pointing — it was an N+1 fan-out in one downstream call. Rather than mandate a fix, I wrote an RFC proposing an SLO per service and a shared latency budget, socialized it 1:1 with each tech lead first so the review wasn't a surprise, and built a reference implementation of the fix in my own service so the pattern was concrete. I set up a shared dashboard and a weekly 15-minute latency sync."
  • R: "Checkout p99 dropped from 1.8s to 600ms, the recurring incident class went to zero, and the SLO/latency-budget model was adopted by two other product areas. I didn't manage anyone involved — it shipped because the data and the shared budget aligned everyone."

How to tell it

Lead with the ambiguity ("no one owned it") and the lack of authority, because that's the SDE3 bar. Emphasize the mechanisms you created (RFC, SLO, dashboard, reference implementation) — those outlast you and show you scale the team, not just yourself. Close with a quantified, durable outcome.
